INTRODUCTION

Aira is an on-demand, autonomous, passenger drone service. The Aira app will allow you to book our electronic vertical take off and landing (eVTOL) vehicles, and provide clear multi-modal booking and navigational features to get you to our vertiports and your destination.

FEATURES

Aira was built to meet user needs and address current pain points with transit. Using a holistic and iterative design process, we were able to narrow our design down to the critical features. We've used Principle, After Effects, and Framer to demonstrate some of the functionality.

USER TESTING

With user research and extensive testing with 44 walkthroughs, we progressed from low-fidelity on to high-fidelity prototyping. We aimed to take our user-driven concept to a professional product with the three testing methods found below.

Contextual Observation

On a Friday, at the end of the workday, we went out to an office of 8 in central Sydney to test our prototype with our target business commuter demographic. By giving them clear tasks relevant to their context, and minimal background information about Aira, we could see how the design held up in the real world. This helped us finetune the placement of additional features like adding car pickup services or cancelling a trip.

Think Alouds

Although our observations were very helpful, we also wanted to do another round of think aloud testing to ensure our design assumptions translated into how users would react to the prototype. We sourced 5 non-design background potential users for this round, a couple of which had been through our low-fidelity think aloud. Although users could accomplish our tasks, some found there needed to be further signifiers of certain interactions.

Design Critiques

We shared our high-fidelity prototypes with design computing peers and experts to get critiques from new perspectives. The professional experts included a couple of interaction designers, a graphic designer, an architect, and a content writer. They pushed our designs to the next level with new interaction models and content design that we had not considered.

ITERATIONS

Implementing all the insights from our user testing, we created 10 iterations of our screens. With our low-fidelity prototypes, our main focus was the information architecture and user flow. As we narrowed down into high fidelity, we iterated the visual layout and interaction models. In each iteration, we were able to better decide the amount of information to provide users paired with the most streamlined decision making flow.

RESULTS

In our final round of user testing, our 8 participants accomplished tasks with ease and found the experience intuitive and visually appealing, displaying the success of a user-driven iterative design process. Our team learned a great deal about our target demographic, what drives positive experiences for them, and how to take those insights from synthesis to a fully realised product. We hope you enjoy Aira as much as ourselves and our users.
